Bali, Indonesia

Island in Indonesia
4.5 (1,238 reviews)
Popular with travelers worldwide

Overview

Bali is a living postcard, an Indonesian paradise that feels like a fantasy. The "Island of the Gods" boasts picturesque beaches, volcanic hillsides, lush rice terraces, and enchanting temples. Bali's rich culture manifests in its arts, dance, music, and food, creating a destination that delights all the senses.

From the spiritual heart of Ubud to the surf beaches of Canggu and the luxury resorts of Nusa Dua, Bali offers diverse experiences for every traveler. The island's warm hospitality and stunning natural beauty make it one of the world's most beloved tropical destinations.

Best Time to Visit

April to October (Dry season)

Language

Balinese, Indonesian (English widely spoken)

Currency

Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)

Top Highlights

Pristine Beaches

From the famous Kuta Beach to hidden gems like Padang Padang, Bali's coastline offers white sand, surf spots, and breathtaking sunsets.

Ancient Temples

Discover spiritual sites like Uluwatu Temple perched on cliffs and the iconic Tanah Lot, a sea temple that appears to float on water.

Tegalalang Rice Terraces

Walk through the stunning emerald-green rice paddies that cascade down the hillsides in Ubud's countryside.

Balinese Cuisine

Savor local dishes like Babi Guling (suckling pig), Bebek Betutu (slow-cooked duck), and the famous Luwak coffee.
